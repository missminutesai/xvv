6.1 Bitcoin Transaction Accelerator. Our Application contains a Replace-By-Fee (“RBF”) feature. The RBF features allows you to replace your unconfirmed Bitcoin transaction with a different version of that transaction that has a higher transaction fee. Further details of the feature and the transaction fees for such feature is available on the Application.

6.2 You understand that if you use the RBF feature, you will be initiating a Bitcoin transfer with a specific fee, flagging it as replaceable and indicating that you may want to update that fee later, before you broadcast the transaction to the Bitcoin Network; if the transaction is taking too long to confirm, you can create a new one with the same inputs, but at a higher fee; on confirmation, the new transaction will invalidate the old one, preventing the possibility of a double spend. Replacement transactions must follow certain rules; you must first signal for RBF by flagging a payment as RBF-enabled when originally broadcasting it; for unconfirmed inputs, you can spend any unspent Bitcoins; your replacement transaction must have a higher fee than the original, and the increase must be at least equal to the minimum relay fee (the lowest fee that the network will accept); your transactions must have the same outputs, as the new transaction sends funds to the same address(es) as before.

6.3 By using the RBF features, you acknowledge and agree that you are familiar with the RBF mechanism and the risk involved. Through RBF, you will be replacing your original payment with a double-spend copy. You understand that there are numerous risks including but not limited to: the main risk for the RBF feature is that there is potential confusion if the recipient sees pending payments that seem to appear and disappear in their wallet, and that extra diligence is needed when leveraging RBF; you could accidentally invalidate your original transactions; the recipient may see the pending transaction appear and disappear from their wallets, which may confuse them; other users may front-run your replacement by offering higher fees; and RBF’s technical complexity may be challenging to set up and lead to mistakes.

What Is Bitlayer?

Bitlayer is a Bitcoin Layer 2 protocol that expands Bitcoin’s capabilities by unlocking smart contract functionality, increasing transaction speeds, and reducing costs.

The L2 comes packed with EVM compatibility and supports Ethereum tooling while utilizing BTC as the native token for gas fees.

Introduced in 2024, Bitlayer offloads and batches transactions together to reduce the work for Bitcoin’s main chain. Thanks to Bitlayer’s Proof of Stake-based consensus protocol, block confirmation takes less than 3 seconds, while the median transaction fee is approximately 10 cents.

Seamlessly Bridge Bitcoin Assets to and From Bitlayer

Bitlayer has implemented a multichain bridge that can communicate with various blockchains, support interoperability, and facilitate cross-chain asset transfers. 

But here is the kicker feature of Bitlayer’s bridge: it allows you to directly transfer assets from centralized exchanges (CEXs) to compatible Bitlayer wallets.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is Bitlayer EVM compatible?

Yes. Bitlayer is 100% compatible with the Ethereum Virtual Machine (EVM, thanks to its Turing-complete BitVM engine, which also supports other virtual machines, including Cairo VM, SolVM, and MoveVM.

The compatibility with EVM is a significant step because it expands Bitcoin’s functionality to include complex smart contracts that can power DeFi and other Web3 applications. That means builders can deploy their Ethereum dApps on Bitcoin and rely on its security all while enjoying faster transaction speeds and lower costs.

Does Bitlayer have a token?

According to Bitlayer’s official website, a native governance token called BTR is in the works, but an official date for a token generation event (TGE) hasn’t been announced yet. However, the Layer 2 project has listed the token’s contract on btrscan.com, where it shows that $BTR will be an ERC-20 token with a total supply of 1 billion.
